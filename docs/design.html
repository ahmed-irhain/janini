Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Keep background cream and ink warm-near-black everywhere — the warmth is load-bearing for the reassuring, not-clinical tone.
  • Ration auroraWash and plumPanel to one decorative use per screen.
  • Set textAlign: right explicitly on Arabic content text.
  • Use weight (Black/Bold vs. Regular/Medium) within ThmanyahSans for hierarchy instead of a second font family.
  • Keep at least one visible source citation on any medical/weekly content card.

Don't

  • Use accent (coral) for primary buttons or navigation — it's a sparing detail color, not a second primary.
  • Introduce diagnostic language or a mood-tracking mascot metaphor — this is tracking-and-information, not mental health.
  • Mirror or re-flow layout logic that assumes LTR reading order.
  • Apply full-pill rounding to dense data tables or list rows.
  • Add a dark-mode palette speculatively before it's actually scoped.
